Challah Bread

Ingredients
1 1/2 C Lukewarm Water
One Packet/2 1/4 Tsp Active Dry Yeast
2 Tsp Sugar
5 Eggs
7 Cups Flour (At least; Not exact)
2 1/2 Tbsp Salt
1/2 Cup Orange Blossom Honey
Canola Oil, for greasing

Tools
2 Medium/Large Mixing Bowls
Spoon
Whisk
Measuring Cups
Oven
Parchment Paper
Rolling Pin
Baking Brush
2 Trays
Knife

Instructions- Mixing
Stir together:
1/4 Cup Water
1 Packet Yeast
2 Teaspoons Sugar
Let sit for 10 minutes
Mixture should turn foamy. If not, get new yeast.

Whisk in:
1 1/4 Cups Water
1 Full Egg
3 Egg Yolks
1/4 Cup Honey
2 Tablespoons Canola Oil
2 Tablespoons Salt
Incorporate everything thoroughly.

Add 4 1/2-6 cups of flour in increments of 1/2 a cup at a time. Stir between increments with a large spoon.
When it becomes too thick to stir, knead with your hands.
Add flour and knead until the dough is smooth and elastic, but not sticky.

Instructions- Dough Work
Grease your second mixing bowl with canola oil.
Move your dough into the new bowl and flip it around to coat it in oil.

If it's warm outside, cover bowl in plastic wrap and sit it by a window to rise.
If it's cold outside, boil a pot of water and place it on the lower oven rack. Cover the bowl in a damp dishcloth and put it on the top oven rack. Close the oven.
Wait 1 hour, or until size doubles.
Punch the dough to remove air.
Wait another hour.
Punch again.

Dust flour on a cutting board (or baking tray, or another smooth surface you can use to roll out dough)
Knead dough on the board. It doesn't need a ton of kneading - just until you can press your finger into it and the indent will bounce back.

Separate the dough into as many parts as loaves you want.

Instructions- More Dough Work
Work one loaf at a time, and set the other parts of dough off to the side.
Divide the dough into three equal parts with a knife.
Roll these parts out with a rolling pin until it is 1/4 inch thick.
With your hands, roll each part up into a strand, sort of like a long cylinder.
Keep pressure on it as you roll so no air pockets collect. Roll it back and forth to smooth it out, and taper a bit at the ends so it's thicker in the middle.

Lay the three strands side by side and braid them.
Start in the middle, then flip it around and do the other side. When you're done, tuck the ends underneath the loaf to make it nice and pretty!

Repeat the steps in this section for as many loaves as you're making.
While you're doing this, if you're letting the dough rise in the oven with a pot of boiling water under it, you should put a pot of water on the stove to boil. You'll need it soon.

Instructions- Finishing Touches and Baking
Mix egg wash in a cup
1 egg
1 Tbsp cold water
1/2 Tbsp salt
1/2 Tbsp honey

Whisk with a fork until smooth.
Brush over the top of the braided loaves. Don't use up all the egg wash, you'll need it later.

Let the loaves rise for 30-45 minutes, or until they bounce back when you press into them.
When you've got about 20 minutes left, take the loaves (and your pot of water) out of the oven (if that's where you have them rising), and start preheating the oven to 350 degrees

Bake for 20 minutes
Remove from oven, coat with more egg wash. Focus on the parts that haven't turned golden.
Bake again for 20 minutes. Total bake time is 40 minutes.

Done! If the challah starts going stale, you can make french toast from it.
